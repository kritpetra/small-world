Barabasi-Albert Model

A group of people walk one by one into a networking event. As each person walks into the room, they exchange contact information with one or two people and continues mingling. As the room gets more crowded, each new person notices how certain individuals are approached more often, and so are more likely to seize the networking opportunity and also give them their contact information. At the end of the event, a small handful of people were able to connect with a lot of people, while the rest has only talked to one or two.

The key feature of the model is its ability to produce hubs, or actors with significantly more connections than the average.
