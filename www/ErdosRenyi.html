Erdos-Renyi Model

You find yourself in a room of people. Bewildered, you all walk around aimlessly, forming connections with random people as you walk past. After some time, the door opens and everybody shuffles out.

This model, of course, while simple and easy to visualize, is problematic in that it does not adequately represent any aspect of real-world social networks.

  1. Look at the degree distribution. Does it appear to be a scale-free network?
  2. What is the highest clustering coefficient you can get with 50 nodes? How does this compare with other models? Does this surprise you?
  3. What about the smallest average degrees of separation? Again, how does this compare with other models?
